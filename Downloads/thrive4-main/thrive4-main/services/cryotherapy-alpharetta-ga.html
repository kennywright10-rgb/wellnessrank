What Is Whole-Body Cryotherapy?

Whole-body cryotherapy (WBC) exposes your body to ultra-cold temperatures — between -200°F and -300°F — for a controlled 2–3 minute session. The rapid cold triggers a cascade of physiological responses: blood is redirected to your core, your body floods with anti-inflammatory cytokines, and when you step out, oxygen-rich blood rushes back to your muscles and joints. The effect is immediate — most patients notice reduced soreness, elevated mood, and better sleep the same day.

At Thrive 4 Peak Performance, every cryotherapy session is supervised by our clinical team under physician oversight — not a staffed spa. Benefits of Cryotherapy

Why elite athletes and everyday performers choose cold therapy

Rapid Inflammation Reduction

Cold exposure triggers a powerful anti-inflammatory response within minutes. Chronic inflammation — the root driver of most pain and metabolic dysfunction — responds well to consistent cryotherapy protocols designed by our physician.

Accelerated Muscle Recovery

Speeds muscle repair after intense training by reducing lactic acid buildup and microtrauma-related inflammation. Many athletes use cryo the day after hard workouts to be back at full capacity faster.

Natural Pain Relief

Cold therapy numbs nerve endings and suppresses pain signals — providing real relief from chronic joint pain, arthritis, and post-surgical soreness without pharmaceuticals or their side effects.

Deeper, More Restorative Sleep

Cryotherapy triggers a significant release of norepinephrine and endorphins that regulate mood and sleep cycles. Patients consistently report falling asleep faster and waking more rested.

Metabolic Boost

The thermal shock activates brown adipose tissue and increases your basal metabolic rate as your body works to warm itself back up — burning additional calories for up to 24 hours post-session.

Mental Clarity & Mood

The norepinephrine surge from cold exposure significantly improves focus, alertness, and emotional resilience. Many patients use cryotherapy as a performance tool for demanding workdays, not just physical recovery.

What to Expect During Your Session

From walk-in to walk-out in under 30 minutes

1

Screening & Prep

Our clinical team reviews your health history to confirm cryotherapy is appropriate. You'll change into minimal, dry clothing — we provide gloves, socks, and slippers. The whole prep takes about 5 minutes.

2

The 2–3 Minute Session

You step into the chamber as nitrogen vapor brings the temperature to between -200°F and -300°F. A team member remains with you and monitors throughout. Most people find it invigorating rather than uncomfortable — you're never alone.

3

Immediate Benefits

You'll feel warmth returning within 30–60 seconds of stepping out, along with an endorphin-driven energy boost. You can go straight back to your day — no downtime, no recovery period needed.

Who Benefits from Cryotherapy?

Great candidates include:

  • Athletes and weekend warriors seeking faster recovery
  • People with chronic joint pain, arthritis, or fibromyalgia
  • Individuals managing chronic inflammation or autoimmune conditions
  • Executives and professionals seeking sharper mental performance
  • Anyone recovering from orthopedic procedures (with physician clearance)

Not recommended for:

  • Uncontrolled high blood pressure
  • Pregnancy
  • Cold allergy (cold urticaria) or Raynaud's disease
  • Severe cardiovascular conditions
  • Open wounds or active skin infections

Medical screening is required before your first session — we'll confirm eligibility at your consultation.

Cryotherapy — Frequently Asked Questions

How cold does the chamber get?

Between -200°F and -300°F (-130°C to -185°C) using nitrogen vapor. Sessions are 2–3 minutes — enough to activate the therapeutic response without any risk to skin tissue.

How many sessions do I need?

Many patients feel benefits after just one session. For chronic inflammation or performance goals, a series of 5–10 sessions over 2–4 weeks typically produces the strongest cumulative effect. Our physician will tailor a recommendation to your situation.

Is it safe?

Yes — when properly supervised. At Thrive 4, all sessions are monitored by our clinical team, and every new patient is screened for contraindications. We don't let people step in without understanding their health history first.

What do I wear?

Minimal, dry clothing — shorts or a swimsuit for maximum exposure. We provide gloves, socks, and foot protection. No wet clothing or metal jewelry allowed in the chamber.

How long does the whole appointment take?

The cryo session itself is 2–3 minutes. Total time in the clinic, including prep and check-in, is under 30 minutes. It fits easily into a lunch break or before/after work.

Can I combine cryotherapy with other services?

Absolutely. Cryotherapy pairs especially well with compression therapy (immediately after for drainage), red light therapy (for cellular recovery), and IV therapy (for systemic anti-inflammatory support). Our physician can design a combined protocol for your goals.
